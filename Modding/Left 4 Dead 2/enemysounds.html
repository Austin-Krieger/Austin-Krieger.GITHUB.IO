Change Boomer Sounds

  1. Locate your Steam folder and continue to the second Left 4 Dead 2 folder like before.

    2. D:\Steam\steamapps\common\Left 4 Dead 2\left4dead2

  2. Continue through the sound and player folders to find a list of characters whos' voice lines we can play around with:

    3. D:\Steam\steamapps\common\Left 4 Dead 2\left4dead2\sound\player

  3. I would like to change the Boomer sound files to that of the Boomer from Gears of War.
  4. Inside the boomer folder you will see a list of folders named: explode, fall, hit, etc.
  5. Open the folder containing the sounds you wish to replace. This will give you an idea of how many sounds you can use when replacing them. If you don't have enough replacement files for the number they have, then you can just copy the files you do have until you come up with the 8 or 15 or whatever you need.
  6. Create a new folder on your desktop or wherever and type in the name of the sound mod you wish to make.
  7. Inside of this folder, we will need to copy the folder structure containing the boomer sound files.
  8. Start by creating a sound folder and within that, create a player folder, and continue on to make a boomer folder.
  9. Inside the boomer folder we need to make the folders containing any files we wish to replace. Referring to the original folder we have a hit folder with boomer_shoved_##.wav files. So then you would create your own hit folder and place your selected sound files inside.
  10. Rename your files boomer_shoved_01.wav, boomer_shoved_02.wav and so on... continue from here until you are satisfied that you have a replacement file for every sound that character makes that you wish to replace.
  11. You can download your sounds from soundboards or wherever, but make sure you convert them to a .wav format. You can use a free software called Audacity for your conversion and editing needs.
  12. Use the following image as an example of what your sound files should look like. For more information on Preparing Audio Files, check the section below.
  13. Remember to make an addinfo.txt just inside your mod folder using the example below:
  14. Package your mod, move it to the addons folder, and get to work testing it out in game.
  15. Additional information on the previous step can be found in the previous pages on this site.

Preparing Audio Files

  1. When you first drag an audio file into Audacity you will likely see this:
  2. The first problem here is that the sound file is set to Stereo which has two tracks. We need to change this to Mono and remove the second track.
  3. Click on the drop down above the Mute and Solo buttons to the left of the audio waves.
  4. Click Split Stereo to Mono.
  5. Next we want to either hit the Mute button on the second track or the X button and remove it completely.
  6. We need to make sure the sound is loud enough by making a selection of the audio as shown below (Click + Drag), and using the Effect > Amplify option near the top of the program. You can use a value between 5 and 7 for some good results here.
  7. One more thing to be aware of is how long the original audio file is compared to the one you want to replace it with.
  8. You can drag the original file into your Audacity project to check sound lengths.
  9. As you can see below, our new sound is just a little longer than the original.
  10. One way to fix this is to make a selection of our sound and hit the delete button, but a better way in this case is to select our entire sound and use the Effect > Change Tempo option. Give it a percentage like 17 or even more if needed and our sound will more closely match with the original.
  11. Lastly you will need to export as a .wav file with the name of the original file and place it in your folder structure.
