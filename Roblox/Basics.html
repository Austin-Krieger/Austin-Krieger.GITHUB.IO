Without anchoring you'll notice that any parts you've added other than a spawn location will fall into empty space.

Anchoring locks parts in place and stops them from moving - they'll even stay in place when players or other objects bump into them.

Anchor parts by selecting them and clicking the Anchor button.

 

In Roblox, studs are the basic measurement unit.

If a part only moved in "steps" or rotates 45 degrees at a time, this is because of snapping. Snapping is useful when moving parts that should be placed exactly together, like the walls of buildings.

To adjust the snap amount, enter a different number for Rotate or Move (or click the small arrows within the fields).

Alternatively, you can turn snapping off by unchecking the box next to Rotate and/or Move.

 

It's a good idea to enter a custom name for the parts you add. This will help you find the part in the Explorer window when you need to change something about it.

Selecting a part in the Explorer window will highlight it in the game view.

  1. Right-click the part's name and select Rename from the context menu.
  2. Type in a better name.
 

In Roblox Studio,the Collisions option lets you control if parts should be blocked from moving into each other. To turn collisions on or off, click the Collisions button.

If you set collisions on, you won't be able to move a part into any position where it overlaps another part. If you set collisions off, you can freely move parts anywhere in the world.

 

Make sure that you're viewing the Model tab. In the Constraints section, click the small arrow below Create and select Hinge.

Move your mouse pointer into the game editor window and hover over the base part. Move your mouse until the (green sphere) is centered on the top surface. Now do the same to the part you want to connect the hinge constraint to.

These constrains appear in the Explorer window and can be moved at any time.

 

It's a good idea to save and test your design regularly. Just click the Play button to start up the game and test what you've built.

Look for areas that can be improved and balanced. If your level is too dificult, players will get frustrated, too easy and they get bored.

 

It's good practice to publish your game to Roblox every 10-15 minutes. In addition to saving your work to a secure place, publishing allows other players on Roblox to play your game.

  1. Select file → Publish to Roblox to open the publishing window.
  2. Enter a name and an optional description.
  3. When ready, click the Create button.
 

In Roblox Studio, click the New button in the upper-left or use an existing project.

Create a Starting Point

In Roblox, a SpawnLocation marks where a player appears in the world at the start of the game or after restarting. Without a spawn location, players might spawn in the middle of nowhere and fall to their doom.

  1. In the Explorer window, hover over Workspace.
  2. Click the circle + button.
  3. Scroll through the drop-down list until you find SpawnLocation and click on it. (The new spawn location will be created at the exact center of your camera view.)
